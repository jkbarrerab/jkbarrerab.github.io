About Me

I am a tenured researcher at the Astronomy Institute of the National Autonomous Univeristy of Mexico (UNAM) in Mexico City. My main scientific interest is the exploration of the scenarios that explain the correlation of physical parameters at kpc scales in galaxies located in the nearby Universe.

To achieve this goal, I work on a novel spectroscopic technique known as Integral Field Spectroscopy (IFS, also known as Hyperspectral cameras). This technique is able to capture the optical spectra from different regions of a galaxy simultaneously. We have employed different analysis techniques on a large sample of galaxies included in different IFS surveys (e.g., CALIFA, MaNGA, AMUSING++).
